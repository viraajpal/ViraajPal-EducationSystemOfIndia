Evolution of State Universities in India

The history of state universities in India dates back to the early 20th century when the first state university, the University of Mysore, was established in 1916. The establishment of state universities was a result of the Indian Education Commission of 1882, which recommended the decentralization of education. The state universities were set up by the state governments to provide affordable and quality education to the students in their respective states. The state universities initially started as affiliating universities and later on started offering their own courses. Today, there are over 400 state universities in India, offering courses in various streams, including arts, science, commerce, engineering, law, medicine, and agriculture. These universities have played a significant role in the growth and development of higher education in the country, producing a large number of graduates who have contributed to the nation's progress.

Savitribai Phule Pune University

Formerly known as the University of Pune, Savitribai Phule Pune University is a state university located in Pune, Maharashtra. It was founded in 1949 and offers courses in various streams, including science, management, engineering, and humanities. The university has been ranked 10th among all universities in India by NIRF in 2021.The institution's name was changed from the University of Pune to Savitribai Phule Pune University on 9 August 2014 in honor of Savitribai Phule, the 19th century Indian social reformer who played an important role in improving the lives of women and the Dalit communities in Maharashtra during British colonial rule. She and her husband Mahatma Jyotiba Phule founded India's first native-run school for girls in 1848.

University of Madras

The University of Madras is a public state university located in Chennai, Tamil Nadu. It was established in 1857, making it one of the oldest universities in India. The university offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and research programs in various disciplines, including science, humanities, social sciences, and management. The University of Madras has been accredited by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) with an "A+" grade. It has been ranked 22nd among all universities in India by NIRF in 2021. The university has a rich history and has produced many renowned alumni, including Nobel laureate Sir C. V. Raman and former Indian President Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan. The University of Madras is the alma mater of five presidents of India, including A. P. J. Abdul Kalam; two Indian physics Nobel laureates, CV Raman and Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar; several notable mathematicians including Srinivasa Ramanujan; Abel Prize winner S. R. Srinivasa Varadhan; and Turing Award winner Raj Reddy among others.

University of Calcutta

The University of Calcutta is a public state university located in Kolkata, West Bengal. It was founded in 1857 and is one of the oldest universities in India. The university offers undergraduate and postgraduate courses in various disciplines, including arts, science, commerce, law, and engineering. It has been ranked 11th among all universities in India by NIRF in 2021. The university has a total of fourteen campuses spread over the city of Kolkata and its suburbs. As of 2020, 151 colleges and 21 institutes and centres are affiliated with CU. The university was fourth in the Indian University Ranking 2021 list, released by the National Institutional Ranking Framework of the Ministry of Education of the Government of India. Its alumni and faculty include several heads of state and government, social reformers, prominent artists, the only Indian Academy award winner and Dirac medal winner, many Fellows of the Royal Society and three Nobel laureates as of 2019. The Nobel laureates associated with this university are C. V. Raman, Amartya Sen, and Abhijit Vinayak Banerjee.
