Evolution of Central Universities in India

Central Universities in India have evolved significantly since the establishment of the University of Calcutta, the first modern university in India, in 1857. Following the University of Calcutta, universities were established in other cities such as Bombay, Madras, and Allahabad in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The first Central University, the University of Delhi, was established in 1922 to provide education at the national level. After independence in 1947, more Central Universities were established to cater to the needs of the newly-formed nation. Today, there are 51 Central Universities in India that are funded and regulated by the Government of India. These universities offer a wide range of academic programs in various fields, including science, arts, humanities, engineering, and management. Central Universities have played a significant role in shaping the education system in India and have produced many renowned scholars, scientists, and leaders who have contributed to the nation's development. The region with the most central universities in India is Delhi with seven universities. There are central universities in all of the states of India except Goa. Of the union territories, there are central universities in Delhi, Jammu and Kashmir, Ladakh and Puducherry.

Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU)

JNU is a top-ranked Central University in India, located in New Delhi. It was established in 1969 and offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and research programs in various fields such as science, arts, humanities, engineering, and management. JNU is known for its high-quality education, research facilities, and vibrant campus culture. It is also renowned for its social and political activism, and many of its alumni have gone on to become leaders in various fields. The president of India is the visitor of the university. The chancellor is the nominal head of the university and the vice-chancellor is the executive head of the university. They are both appointed by the visitor on the recommendations of the Executive Council. The Court, the Executive Council, the Academic Council and the Finance Committee are the administrative authorities of the university.

University of Hyderabad

The University of Hyderabad is a prestigious Central University located in Hyderabad, Telangana. It was established in 1974 and offers a wide range of academic programs in science, arts, humanities, and social sciences. The university is known for its excellent research facilities and has produced many notable scientists and scholars. It also has a diverse student community and offers a multicultural learning environment. The university campus is on 2300-odd acres, and has a large area under forest cover, with two perennial and three seasonal lakes, and rock formations characteristic of the Deccan. It is home to 734 plants, 10 mammals, 15 reptiles and 220 birds species (at last count). Notable animals include spotted deer, hares, peacocks, porcupines and wild boar. The campus also has a protected megalithic site.

Banaras Hindu University (BHU)

BHU is a top-ranked Central University located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh. It was established in 1916 and is one of the oldest and largest universities in India. BHU offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and research programs in various fields such as science, arts, humanities, and engineering. The university is known for its excellent faculty, research facilities, and vibrant campus culture. It has produced many renowned scholars, scientists, and leaders who have contributed to the development of the country. Banaras Hindu University's main campus is located on the southern edge of Varanasi, near the banks of the river Ganges. Development of the main campus, spread over 1,370 acres (5.5 km2),[46][47] started in 1916 on land donated by the then Kashi Naresh Prabhu Narayan Singh. The campus layout approximates a semicircle, with intersecting roads laid out along the radii or in arcs. Buildings built in the first half of the 20th century are fine examples of Indo-Gothic architecture.
